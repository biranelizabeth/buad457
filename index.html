A Field Guide to
Creative Problem Solving

William & Mary  ·  Lessons from One Semester

This is not a textbook. It is a field guide assembled from a semester of games that felt more meaningful than lectures, conversations that changed how I listen, and moments that quietly rearranged my assumptions. What began as weekly blog entries became something I didn't expect: a coherent map of how I think, where I get stuck, and what I want to carry forward. Three discoveries kept surfacing across every exercise, every guest speaker, every chaotic classroom activity. This website is my attempt to share them as lived lessons with practical roots. I hope they challenge something in you, too.

The Discoveries

Chapter I

Embrace the Unknown

On ambiguity, adaptability, and letting go of control

Creative thinking begins exactly where certainty ends. This chapter explores what happens when you stop fighting uncertainty and start treating it as the actual material to work with.

Listen to Learn

On curiosity, collaboration, and knowledge without agenda

We are trained to absorb information in order to perform with it. This chapter is about unlearning that reflex and discovering how much richer thinking becomes when curiosity is freed from any obligation to be useful.

Reframe Everything

On perspective shifts, cognitive reappraisals, and new seeing

The most powerful creative tool isn't a framework or a method. It's perspective. This chapter is about the subtle, transformative practice of asking: what if I thought about this differently?

How to Use This Guide

Each chapter opens with a summary of the theme, followed by three lessons drawn from class activities, guest speakers, and personal reflection. Every lesson ends with a note on how I'm actually working to apply it. You don't need to read in order. Start wherever the title resonates most.
